Find the smallest difference (positive integer) between two arrays:

Example: smallest difference is 5 calculated from 8 and 3 

                [12, 56, 3, 90, 67]
                [45, 34, 98, 8]

Approach:

Inputs are two arrays and output is the min difference between any pairs from the 2 arrays. A naive approach is to find the difference by subtracting each element from the two arrays.

What do we know about the difference between two numbers? The difference between two numbers is smallest the closer they are positioned. For instance, diff between 8 and 5 is smaller than diff between 90 and 8. We start by sorting the two arrays.

How do we find the smallest difference between two sorted arrays? Let's think about how we merge two sorted arrays. We compare each element from both arrays one by one then we increment our pointer from the smallest side. Similarly here, we calculate the difference between two elements from each array then increment the smallest pointer. Update the min diff as we go.
